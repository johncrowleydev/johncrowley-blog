Dimensional analysis is often introduced in school as a technique for physics homework. Then it gets mentally filed away with a thousand other useful things people vaguely remember but do not actively practice.

That is a mistake. Dimensional analysis is not just an academic tool. It is a way of thinking that protects intuition from becoming sloppy.

## Units are structure, not decoration

A number without units often carries a dangerous illusion of completeness. It feels finished because it is precise. But precision without dimensional context can be misleading.

Is the quantity a rate? An area? An energy? A probability? A mass per unit volume? The units tell you what kind of thing the number is allowed to be.

## A sanity check for intuition

One reason dimensional analysis is so valuable is that it disciplines intuition without killing it. It does not replace understanding. It acts as a guardrail around understanding.

A rough model may still be rough, but if the units refuse to reconcile, the model is telling on itself.
